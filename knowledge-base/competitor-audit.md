# Competitor Audit Framework

**Purpose:** Systematic analysis of competitor conversion approaches to identify learnings, gaps and opportunities for your client
**When to run:** At onboarding for every new client; quarterly refresh recommended
**Output:** Competitor audit report fed into hypothesis generation

---

## 1. Why Competitor Analysis Matters for CRO

Optimising in isolation means missing industry-wide shifts in user expectations. Competitors who convert better have already run the tests and found the answers. Analysing their approach tells you:
- What patterns users in this vertical are now conditioned to expect
- Where your client is behind the market standard
- Where differentiation opportunities exist (what nobody is doing well)
- What tests are worth prioritising based on what is already working elsewhere

As Invesp notes: without competitor analysis, you are optimising in the dark. You could unknowingly make changes that drive customers to competitors with more compelling strategies.

## 2. What To Analyse

### 2.1 Funnel Structure
For each competitor, map:
- Entry points (homepage, landing pages, paid ads)
- Primary CTA and placement
- Number of steps from landing to conversion
- Where they ask for commitment (early vs late in journey)
- Checkout or form flow structure

### 2.2 Value Proposition
- Hero headline — outcome vs feature vs brand led?
- Subheadline — what reassurance is provided?
- Key differentiators — what do they lead with?
- Pricing transparency — shown upfront or gated?
- Free trial / freemium — how is it positioned?

### 2.3 Trust Signals
- Types of social proof used (reviews, logos, stats, testimonials)
- Placement of trust signals (hero, near CTA, below fold?)
- Credentials displayed (accreditations, awards, press)
- Review platform integration (G2, Trustpilot, Google)

### 2.4 Urgency and Scarcity
- What urgency signals are used?
- Are they authentic or manufactured?
- How prominent are they?

### 2.5 Mobile Experience
- How does the mobile flow compare to desktop?
- Is the CTA accessible without scrolling on mobile?
- Is the checkout/form mobile-optimised?

### 2.6 Pricing and Package Structure
- How many tiers?
- Is one highlighted as recommended/popular?
- Annual vs monthly billing default?
- How are features presented (table, icons, bullet list)?

### 2.7 Content and Copy Patterns
- Tone of voice — formal vs informal vs playful?
- Reading level and length
- Use of video, interactive elements
- FAQ placement and depth

## 4. The Audit Scoring Matrix

Score each competitor across 10 dimensions (0–10 each, max 100):

| Dimension | What to score | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Value proposition clarity | Is it immediately clear what they do and why? | High |
| CTA clarity and prominence | Single, clear, action-oriented? | High |
| Social proof quality | Specific, credible, well-placed? | High |
| Trust signals | Relevant credentials, recognisable logos? | High |
| Mobile experience | Accessible, fast, thumb-friendly? | High |
| Form/checkout friction | Minimal fields, logical flow, no surprises? | Medium |
| Pricing transparency | Clear, honest, early in funnel? | Medium |
| Urgency authenticity | Real and specific vs manufactured? | Medium |
| Content quality | Helpful, outcome-focused, readable? | Low |
| Page speed | Visually fast, no layout shift? | Low |

## 5. What To Do With The Findings

### Gap Analysis
Where is your client scoring significantly below the competitor average? These are priority areas.

### Differentiation Opportunities
Where are all competitors weak? These are chances to lead the market.

### Hypothesis Generation
Each gap becomes a potential test hypothesis:

```
Competitor analysis finding:
  "3 of 4 competitors show delivery cost on product page; 
   client only shows at checkout"

Becomes hypothesis:
  "If we show delivery cost on product pages,
   then checkout abandonment will decrease
   because surprise costs are the top abandonment trigger"
```

### Pattern Recognition
If all competitors in a vertical use the same pattern (e.g. all show "Most Popular" on pricing pages), it signals user expectation. Deviating requires strong evidence. Matching is table stakes.

AUDITOR: [your name]

FUNNEL MAP:
  Entry: [URL / ad]
  Step 1: [page + primary CTA]
  Step 2: [page + primary CTA]
  Conversion: [what counts as conversion]

VALUE PROPOSITION:
  Hero headline: [exact copy]

  Key differentiator: [what they lead with]
  
TRUST SIGNALS:
  Type: [reviews / logos / stats / testimonials]
  Placement: [above fold / near CTA / below fold / footer]

  Number of tiers: [X]

MOBILE:

SCORES:
  Value proposition: [0–10]

## 7. Competitor Audit Cadence

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| New client onboarding | Full audit of top 3–5 competitors |
| Quarterly review | Rescan top 2 competitors for changes |
| Before a BIG test | Check if competitors have already tested this pattern |
| After a test loss | Check if competitors do it differently |
| Client reports competitor threat | Immediate full audit of named competitor |
